Why Does Apple Music Keep Opening on Mac?

If Apple Music keeps opening automatically on your Mac, you're not alone. Apple designed Music.app to launch in response to media events — and there's no built-in setting to turn it off.

AirPods & Bluetooth

Connecting AirPods or any Bluetooth headphones triggers Apple Music to launch and sometimes auto-play.

Play/Pause Key

Pressing the media play/pause key on your keyboard or Touch Bar opens Music, even if you use Spotify.

Handoff from iPhone

If Handoff is enabled, ending a phone call or switching audio can wake Music.app on your Mac.

Random Auto-Launch

Some users report Apple Music opening completely unprompted — at startup, after sleep, or seemingly at random.

Apple considers this intentional behavior, not a bug. There's no checkbox in System Settings to disable it. That's why No More Music exists.

How It Works

No More Music is a tiny, compiled Swift binary that uses macOS native APIs to permanently prevent Apple Music from opening on your Mac.

1

Listens for app launches

Uses NSWorkspace.didLaunchApplicationNotification — the same API macOS uses internally. Zero CPU while waiting.

2

Detects Music.app instantly

The moment Apple Music tries to open — from any trigger — the binary is notified by the system.

3

Kills it before it loads

Music.app is terminated immediately. No window appears. No audio plays. It never fully launches.

4

Runs forever in the background

A macOS LaunchAgent starts it at login and keeps it running. Survives restarts. Set it and forget it.

Frequently Asked Questions

Why does Apple Music keep opening automatically on my Mac?

Apple Music auto-launches in response to media events: connecting AirPods or Bluetooth headphones, pressing the play/pause key, Handoff from iPhone, or macOS restarting with "Reopen windows" enabled. Apple considers this intentional behavior, which is why there is no built-in setting to disable it.

How do I permanently stop Apple Music from opening on Mac?

Install No More Music with one command: git clone https://github.com/beausterling/kill-apple-music.git && cd kill-apple-music && bash install.sh. It runs silently in the background, catches Music.app the instant it tries to launch, and kills it. Works across restarts.

Does this work on macOS Sequoia, Sonoma, and Ventura?

Yes. No More Music works on macOS 12 (Monterey) and later, including Ventura, Sonoma, and Sequoia. It compiles from Swift source on your machine, so it's always compatible with your macOS version and CPU — Intel or Apple Silicon.

Will this stop Apple Music from opening when I connect AirPods?

Yes. No More Music catches Apple Music the instant it tries to open, regardless of the trigger — AirPods, Bluetooth headphones, the play/pause key, Handoff, or any other cause. Music.app is killed before it fully loads.

How is this different from noTunes?

No More Music is event-driven — it uses macOS native notifications to react instantly, using zero CPU while idle. It compiles natively on your machine, requires no menu bar icon, and installs with a single terminal command. noTunes is a great app too, but it uses polling and requires Homebrew or a manual download.

How do I uninstall it?

Run ~/.kill-apple-music/uninstall.sh in Terminal. This removes the background agent and all installed files. Apple Music will launch normally again immediately.
